Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

The most famous signers include:

John Hancock
The President of the Continental Congress, best known for his large and stylish signature which made his name a common synonym for a signature.
Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration of Independence and later the third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A world-renowned scientist, inventor, and diplomat; he was the oldest delegate to sign the document at age 70.
John Adams
A driving political force for independence who served on the drafting committee and later became the second U.S. President.
Samuel Adams
A leader of the Massachusetts "Sons of Liberty" and a cousin to John Adams, known for his passionate opposition to British taxation.
Roger Sherman
A Connecticut statesman and the only person to sign all four of the United States' major founding documents.
